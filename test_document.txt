The rapid advancement of artificial intelligence technologies has fundamentally transformed the landscape of digital media and communication. In recent years, generative models have demonstrated unprecedented capabilities in producing coherent, contextually relevant, and logically structured text that closely mimics human authorship. This evolution poses significant challenges for information integrity, as distinguishing between human-written and machine-generated content becomes increasingly difficult. Consequently, researchers are actively developing sophisticated heuristics, statistical analyses, and machine learning classifiers to detect subtle patterns indicative of algorithmic generation, such as diminished variance in sentence length, predictable vocabulary distribution, and characteristic structural uniformities.
